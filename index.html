Hello World!

Hi,

I am Serda! You did not ask, but do you know whats my favorite thing in the world? Would you like to know? I will tell you anyway because, well, I am hoping it is your favorite too. If it isnt, at least you are using it right now to read this. My favorite tool in the whole world is a computer (laptop, desktop, phone, Arduino, Raspberry Pi etc.). If it has ram, storage, and processor and can be programmed, I am in.

 I received my very first computer at the age of 18. Even by the late 1990s standards, this would be considered insanely late. And yet, it was April of 2004. Why so late you say, the Apple I was introduced in 1976. Well, I was only born in 85! As we get to know each other, I am sure you will understand. Since that day in April of 2004, I have had a boundless appreciation for computers and technology overall. I consistently try to learn and wrap my head around every aspects of computing. I find the mathematics behind it fascinating. I love building small computer networks and have learn a lot about large networks that spans across continents. Software development is where I spend most of my time while using a computer. See Computers are the only item I can think of that when instructed correctly, will always produce the right result. Try that with humans, animals, chemistry, physics, and you would be surprised. Even math as an exact science can take you to wild places. Computers, well, they can too when not properly instructed. Sometimes, small unintended mishaps in software development have yielded great results. Bill Gates had a such experience with floating points with an ALTAIR. But for the most part, almost always, when you instruct a computer, it will certain yield the appropriate result. Further, I enjoy reading about the advances in hardware development, designs, new tech trends and more. **Frankly, at any given point in my life, I want to improve and apply my understanding of computers, software, hardware for the betterment of society and The People.** I want to successfully develop and deploy technology to areas where society can most benefit from it. Pushing the boundaries of possibility. Using computers as a mechanism for progress.

On this site, I will share my experience and experiments with technology. You will hopefully learn something. I will try to keep it entertaining at the same time. The subject can be boring for most. But it is nonetheless intriguing. I have yet to find one person under 50(as of 2019) who wasnt interested in understanding computers. When I started college, professor used to say: Computers are the future. I always replied that adage belongs in 1984. Computers are not the future, they are the past, present, and the future. At some point in my lifetime, Computing would be described as OMNIPRESENT. We may not even remember which model came first. We may not even know the correct story about the machine invented out of the mind of Turing. The more I learn, the more I will share. And hopefully, you will gain insight/warnings. Hopefully, this will help better appreciate the devices in your pocket, hands, backpack, home, car, etc. Hopefully you too, will come to feel this special interest, and boundless love for computers. I just thought my boundless love for computers and computing ought to be shared with the rest of the world. Hence, this is my only goal for this site.

So welcome and join me in this wonderful journey. I promise you; the sky is limit when it comes to computing/imagination.

Best,

Serda
